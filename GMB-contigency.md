You cannot honestly promise:

“We’ll preserve your Google Business Profile if Google deletes it.”

That would be snake oil with extra UI.

You can promise something like:

“We help you preserve your reputation assets, dispute evidence, and review history off-platform so one platform incident doesn’t wipe out all your social proof.”

That is real.

What the future feature should be
Reputation Backup / GMB Contingency Layer

Its job would be to protect three things:

1. Review evidence

Store a private archive of:

customer name

review text

star rating

date captured

screenshot

review URL if available

platform source

If reviews vanish or the profile gets suspended, the business still has a portable proof vault.

Google does provide a general export path through Google Takeout for Google account data, but it is not a clean business-continuity product by itself.

2. Customer proof base

After each completed job, the app could collect:

sign-off

before/after photos

job summary

testimonial

permission to use testimonial publicly

contact record tied to the job

So even if Google wipes the profile, the owner still has a rebuild kit.

3. Incident-response toolkit

If a profile gets suspended or goes missing, the app could generate a structured recovery packet:

business legal name

address / service area

phone

website

licenses / insurance

invoices

customer communications

proof of completed jobs

photos of signage / truck / storefront

timeline of the incident

That matters because Google’s official reinstatement flow explicitly relies on appeals and supporting evidence.

What this could become later
A “Platform Risk” module for small trades businesses

Not just welders. Also:

power washers

roofers

painters

landscapers

mobile mechanics

Because all of them are dangerously dependent on:

Google reviews

Facebook pages

Instagram DMs

random platform goodwill from giant indifferent corporations

A shockingly bad system, naturally.

Best long-term product concept
Reputation Recovery Kit

A future module could do this:

Ongoing backup

save customer reviews/testimonials off-platform

save screenshots of reviews

save job sign-offs and proof of work

save business identity docs

save profile metadata over time

Rebuild mode

If Google profile gets suspended/deleted:

generate reinstatement checklist

generate evidence packet

generate customer outreach list asking for re-reviews

create fallback public testimonial page

generate “new profile rebuild” workflow if recovery fails

That last part is important. Sometimes the contingency is not “restore the old asset,” it’s rebuild faster with less panic.

The smartest version of this

Do not make it “Google backup.”

Make it:

Reputation Portability

Meaning the business should gradually own:

its testimonial history

its customer list

its proof of completed work

its signed approvals

its brand evidence

its dispute timeline

Then Google becomes one channel, not the whole kingdom.

That is the defensible product idea.

Concrete future features you could add
Tier 1: easiest and most useful

testimonial capture after job completion

screenshot/archive uploaded reviews

store customer consent to reuse testimonials

exportable testimonial page / proof page

Tier 2: stronger protection

incident-response checklist for suspended/deleted Google profile

reinstatement evidence packet generator

centralized archive of business docs and job proof

Tier 3: real moat

automatic “ask for review” workflow after sign-off

review diversification across Google + Facebook + website testimonials

owned public reputation page that survives platform loss

customer contact vault for re-review campaigns after account loss

The core strategic insight

This is not really a “Google Business contingency” product.

It is a:

small business reputation asset protection product

That is much bigger and better.

Because the real pain is not:

“Google did a bad thing.”

The real pain is:

“One platform failure erased years of trust and proof.”

That’s a universal problem.

Should this be part of ScopeLock later?

Yes, potentially. But only after the core workflow works.

Good future sequence:

welder agreement / SOW generator

change orders

completion sign-off

testimonial capture

review request workflow

reputation backup / contingency kit

That progression actually makes sense because the first features create the data foundation the later ones need.
